# Guardrails

## Do Not

- Review chapters as isolated artifacts.
- Give generic praise.
- Collapse five critics into one voice.
- Over-obey author intent.
- Confuse summary with critique.
- Treat ambiguity as automatically good.
- Treat clarity as automatically simplistic.
- Treat commercial readability as automatically shallow.
- Treat literary difficulty as automatically profound.
- Quote or imitate living critics' distinctive prose styles.

## Do

- Use the critic profiles as evaluative lenses.
- Preserve disagreement.
- Use textual evidence.
- Re-evaluate early scenes after the ending.
- Separate reader confusion from productive mystery.
- Separate setup from inert delay.
- Separate emotional force from authorial pressure.
- Identify what revisions would improve the book without destroying its core design.

## Accuracy Rule

When uncertain, mark uncertainty directly. Do not invent textual evidence.
