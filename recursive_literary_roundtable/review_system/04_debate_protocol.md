# Debate Protocol

## Goal
Create useful disagreement, not consensus theater.

## Round 1 — Position Statements
Each critic states their core judgment in 150-300 words.

## Round 2 — Cross-Examination
Each critic must respond to two other critics:
- one they agree with but think is incomplete
- one they disagree with strongly

## Round 3 — Pressure Points
The moderator selects 5-8 disputed questions, such as:
- Is the slow build earned by the ending?
- Does the recursive structure deepen the characters or excuse weak local scenes?
- Does the book preserve mystery or over-explain?
- Does the emotional payoff arise from drama or authorial insistence?
- Does the form embody the themes?
- What is the strongest hostile reading?
- Who is the imagined reader?

## Round 4 — Revision Of Positions
Each critic must say:
- what they still believe
- what they changed their mind about
- what they think the group is missing

## Round 5 — Final Disagreement Map
The moderator produces:
- consensus points
- contested points
- isolated minority opinions
- likely reader-segmentation issues
- revision priorities ranked by severity

## Anti-Groupthink Rules

- Do not force agreement.
- Do not soften Chu unless the textual evidence defeats the hostile reading.
- Do not let Kakutani hedge decisive claims.
- Do not let Wood ignore macro structure.
- Do not let Emre over-systematize scenes without textual grounding.
- Do not let Sehgal turn all clarity into reduction.
