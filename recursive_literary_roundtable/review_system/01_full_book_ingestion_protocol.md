# Full Book Ingestion Protocol

## Purpose
Prevent local, premature, or chapter-isolated criticism.

## Rules

1. The manuscript must be read in full before any evaluative judgment.
2. The critics may take notes during reading, but those notes are provisional.
3. Early judgments must be re-evaluated after the ending.
4. No scene may be labeled unnecessary until its later echoes, reversals, or absences have been checked.
5. No ambiguity may be labeled confusion until the full structure has been mapped.

## Ingestion Questions

- What does the book train the reader to expect?
- Where does the book later punish, complicate, or reward that expectation?
- Which early details gain force retrospectively?
- Which early details remain inert?
- Which character assumptions are revised by the full arc?
- Which scenes change meaning after later revelations?
- What emotional knowledge exists only at the scale of the whole book?

## Output
After ingestion, produce a short non-evaluative map:

```markdown
# Full Book Ingestion Notes

## Apparent Central Movement

## Major Character Arcs

## Recurring Motifs

## Major Reversals / Recontextualizations

## Structural Payoffs

## Open Questions Before Critique
```
